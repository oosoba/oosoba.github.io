Future generations may look back at our time and identify it as one of intense change. In a few short decades, we have morphed from a machine-based society to an information-based society, and as this Information Age continues to mature, society has been forced to develop a new and intimate familiarity with data-driven and algorithmic systems. We use the term artificial agents to refer to devices and decisionmaking aids that rely on automated, datadriven, or algorithmic learning procedures (including artificial intelligence (AI) in its many manifestations). 1 These include devices as banal as Roomba robots and online recommendation engines to more advanced cognitive systems like IBM’s Watson. Such agents are becoming an intrinsic part of our regular decisionmaking processes. Their emergence and adoption lead to a bevy of related policy questions.
